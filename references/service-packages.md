# Service Packages

Use these packages when positioning this skill as a service, agency workflow, or paid consulting offer.

Do not present all packages by default. Choose one based on client maturity.

## Package 1: Starter Audit

Best for:
- First-time sellers
- Small merchants
- Low-risk trial clients

What to include:
- One product scorecard
- One platform recommendation
- One offer angle
- One copy pack
- One publish checklist

Suggested positioning:
- Fast diagnosis
- Clear next action
- Low commitment

Best use:
- Lead-in service
- Entry offer

## Package 2: Launch Sprint

Best for:
- Sellers with one active product
- Clients who need content and listing preparation

What to include:
- One product scorecard
- One primary platform plan
- One secondary platform adaptation
- Full copy pack
- Browser-ready field values
- One daily review template for follow-up

Suggested positioning:
- Get a product ready for live testing
- Reduce setup time and copy confusion

Best use:
- Main paid package

## Package 3: Weekly Growth Ops

Best for:
- Operators already posting
- Shops with weekly iteration needs

What to include:
- Weekly product / angle review
- Revised hooks and CTA options
- Fresh copy variants
- Platform posting checklists
- Daily review rollup
- Prioritized next-step recommendations

Suggested positioning:
- Ongoing optimization
- Repeatable weekly operating cadence

Best use:
- Retainer

## Package 4: Multi-Platform Expansion

Best for:
- Clients already converting on one platform
- Businesses expanding to second or third channels

What to include:
- Primary platform baseline
- Secondary and tertiary platform adaptations
- Platform-specific copy packs
- Funnel mapping by platform
- Risk review before scaling

Suggested positioning:
- Expand without losing message clarity
- Reuse one core offer across channels

Best use:
- Higher-ticket package

## Pricing Logic

Use pricing based on:
- Number of products
- Number of platforms
- Whether browser execution is included
- Whether iterative review is included

Simple internal rule:
- Audit = low ticket
- Launch sprint = mid ticket
- Weekly ops = recurring
- Expansion = high ticket

Do not promise revenue outcomes. Sell speed, clarity, workflow, and operational leverage.
